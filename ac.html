Servicing, Repair, and Re-Gas of Your Car’s Air Conditioning System
At Alan Thompson Motor Engineers, we recommend having your car’s air conditioning system serviced with gas and lubricant every two years to keep it running efficiently.

Car Air Conditioning

Air conditioning isn’t just for hot summer days—it’s essential for defogging windows during winter. That’s why it’s vital your car’s air conditioning unit works efficiently all year round. We recommend having your system serviced with gas and lubricant every two years.

Regular checks are crucial because air conditioning isn’t covered by your car’s annual service. Most vehicles over two years old can benefit from a re-gas.

WHAT TO LOOK OUT FOR?

• Temperature dropping
• Unusual smells from the system
• Loss of cooling efficiency
• Engine working harder and using more fuel
• Blowing cold air inconsistently
• Musty odours from the air conditioning system

Often, blowing cold air is the first sign that your car needs an air conditioning re-gas. A musty smell after the system has been idle could indicate the need for an anti-bacterial clean. Our qualified technicians check the lubricant and refrigerant levels to restore your system’s fresh, efficient performance.

  • Use the air conditioning system regularly—aim for at least 20 minutes twice a week, even in winter—to help maintain optimal performance.
  • Annual treatment with an anti-bacterial/anti-odour solution prevents unpleasant smells and improves air quality inside your car.
  • Keep the condenser in front of the radiator clean and free of debris to ensure efficient heat dispersion.
  • Have your system serviced by a specialist every two years, including leak tests, filter replacement, and refrigerant top-ups.
